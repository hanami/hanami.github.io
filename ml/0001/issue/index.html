An intense month
This last month was full of events. First of all, the annoucement of Lotus, then the release of Lotus::Router and Lotus::Utils.

I'm learning a lot of things, about code, about Open Source, about talking with you. People are enthused over this project, and they are providing precious feedbacks. We are experimenting new ideas, validating others, together. This is the most exciting thing of being part of a vibrant Community like this.

Sadly, our beloved Jim Weirich passed away. According to all the people who had the luck to met him, he was a kind person. I had this luck during a conference in London: he was a source of inspiration for me.
Rest in peace, Jim.

Luca Guidi

Getting started
Do you want to start using Lotus::Router but you don't know how to do? I have prepared a gist complete of both integration and unit testing with RSpec. A few lines of code, and you'll be using one of the fastest HTTP routers for Ruby.
Speaking of speed..
Luis Lavena has run benchmarks for all the Ruby's web frameworks. He wrote some basic Hello World apps and compared speed results: Lotus::Router is one of the fastest.

Lotus::Controller
On February 23rd I will open source Lotus::Controller. It's designed to be speed and testable. It's compatible with Rack, and offers callbacks, exceptions management, cookies, sessions, automatic mime types.
